Data optimization and consolidation

Introduction


Over the years, MBFS has accumulated tons of cubes, reports, and dashboards within its Management Reporting environment.(ADD MORE)

Goals


  • Conduct impactful analysis to make insightful recommendation on overall consolidation and optimization of data
  • Write code that can be used to run monthly inspections on data

Analysis


I wanted to get a quick, overview of the data in the environment so I first looked at what kind of reports were being used by employees the most.

I then dived deeper into the analysis and looked into multiple factors involved such as the elements and metrics, usage, run time, and source.

Specifically, I was interested in the execution time of reports based on whether they successfully ran or not. To do this, I divided the Report Usage table based on the "Status" column which consisted of two values, Success and Failure, and conducted statistical analysis for each subgroup. Initially, the statistics I was getting didn't seem quite accurate but with a bit of filtering, I found that a small portion of each subset of the execution times (7-8%) exceeded 5 minutes and was heavily skewing my data. I decided it was best to eliminate those values.


Surface level analysis showed that both distributions were still heavily skewed right. For the failed report executions distribution, there were two obvious spikes at two distinct places, one between 0-0.5 minutes and one between 1.5 and 2.5 minutes.

Upon further investigation, at the:

  • Less than 0.5 minutes spike - Only 9% of the failures were due to user cancellation
  • Between 1.5 and 2.5 minutes spike â€“ 81% of the failures were due to user cancellation

This means that most reports, if failed, fail quite early on while failures later on are usually the result of users manually cancelling the running of the report.
